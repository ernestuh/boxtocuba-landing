### Service Overview
BOX TO CUBA offers package shipping services from Canada to all provinces in Cuba. However, due to the current suspension of commercial flights between Canada and Cuba, we are temporarily limiting our service to deliveries in Havana and Matanzas only.

### Third-Party Agency Services

- This is due to the shared logistics network

### Origin Locations

- **Coverage**: Can receive cargo from any city or country for shipment to Cuba

## 4. Permitted Items and Restrictions

### Package vs. Products Definition
- **Package/Shipment**: The wrapper or container grouping multiple items
- **Products/Articles**: The individual contents within a package

### Quantity Limitations

**General Principles:**
- Personal effects shipping is based on product diversity
- Quantities must not suggest commercial intent
- Some products (appliances) have explicitly defined quantity limits
- Other products (miscellaneous, food) follow the non-commercial principle

**Important Notes:**
- Monitor quantities to avoid customs issues and potential confiscation
- Review detailed product lists: https://avsup.info/app/aduanalist.php

### Maximum Package Weight
- **Miscellaneous items**: Maximum 50kg
- **Appliances and listed items**: Limit based on customs value, not weight
- Reference: https://avsup.info/app/aduanalist.php

### Types of Permitted Articles

**Allowed Categories:**
- Food products
- Medicines
- Appliances
- Vehicle parts and pieces
- General miscellaneous items
- Electrical generation equipment
- Hardware items
- Other items per customs regulations

**Prohibited by Civil Aviation:**
- Weapons
- Flammable products
- Aerosols or compressed gases
- Full list: https://avsup.info/app/uploads/Forbidden-Items.pdf

### Appliances and Equipment

**Value Limit Rule:**
- Total customs value in one package cannot exceed $500 USD
- **Example 1**: TV over 42" has $500 USD customs value = maximum 1 unit per package
- **Example 2**: TV 32" has $250 USD customs value = maximum 2 units per package
- Reference: https://avsup.info/app/aduanalist.php

### Photovoltaic and Renewable Energy Products

**Permitted Without Quantity Limits:**
- Solar generators
- Solar water heaters
- Photovoltaic pumps
- Small wind generators
- Geomembrane biodigesters
- Biogas motor pumps
- Solar lighting solutions
- Solar air conditioning systems

**Special Conditions:**
- Must be packed and shipped separately from other products
- Exempt from tariff payments
- Must be for non-commercial use

### Electronics (TVs, Phones, Computers, Tablets)

**Shipping Requirements:**
- **Allowed**: Yes, but considered high-risk items
- **Packaging**: Must be properly packed and insured
- **Quantity**: Review permitted quantities to avoid customs issues
- Reference: https://avsup.info/app/aduanalist.php

### Automobiles

**Complete Vehicles:**
- **NOT PERMITTED** under personal effects shipping

**Auto Parts and Pieces:**
- **Allowed**: Yes, in reasonable quantities for vehicle repair/maintenance
- **Example Limitation**: Maximum 5 tires per person (sufficient for one vehicle with spare)
- **Multiple Vehicles**: If recipient has 2 vehicles requiring 8 tires, must split shipment to 2 different recipients
- **Customs Rule**: Quantity must not exceed what one vehicle requires for repair and maintenance

**Not Permitted:**
- Complete motor vehicles
- Vehicle engines
- Vehicle bodies
- These items subject to specific legislation

### Motorcycles and Electric Bicycles

**Electric Motorcycles:**
- Any capacity permitted
- Maximum 2 units per person

**Internal Combustion Motorcycles:**
- Maximum 1 unit every 5 years per person
- Must be new, in original crates
- Maximum capacity: 250cc
- Tariffs payable in USD using available banking mechanisms in Cuba

**Important:** Stay updated with current customs regulations

### Electric Generators (Battery and Gasoline)

**Current Regulations (Valid until September 30, 2025):**
- Any generation capacity permitted
- Maximum 1 unit per person
- **Note**: This is a temporary facility - verify current regulations before shipping

### Medicines and Medical Supplies

**Permitted Conditions:**
- Quantities must not suggest commercial intent
- Must be sealed in original containers
- **Prohibited**: Bulk items, loose pills/capsules, products repackaged in other bottles

**Medical Supplies:**
- Must be on customs-approved list
- **Note**: These are temporary facilities - verify current status before shipping

### Mixing Product Types in Packages

**Tariff Implications:**
- **Tariff-Exempt Products**: Food, medicines, personal hygiene products
- **If Mixed**: Combining tariff-exempt with taxable products makes entire package taxable

**Tariff-Exempt Requirements:**
- Food, medicines, and hygiene products can be mixed together
- Maximum weight: 20kg per package
- Must travel separately from other merchandise
- Meeting these conditions = no tariff payment required

### Food/Medicine Packages Exceeding 20kg

**Consequences:**
- Packages exceeding 20kg lose tariff exemption
- Will be processed as miscellaneous package and pay tariffs
- **Recommendation**: If tariff-exempt products represent small portion compared to taxable items, better to ship together as one miscellaneous package

### Chargeable Weight

**Definition:**
- The greater of either:
  1. Physical weight (measured on scale)
  2. Volumetric weight (calculated from package dimensions)

**For Air Shipments:**
- Chargeable weight is always used for billing
- Sales agent will weigh and measure package to determine chargeable weight

## 5. Tariffs and Exemptions

### What Are Customs Tariffs?
Customs tariffs are taxes charged to nationalize products and permit entry into the destination country.

### Tariff Calculation

**Calculation Methods:**
- Based on product weight OR customs-established price
- **Example 1**: Miscellaneous items - calculated by weight
- **Example 2**: Appliances - based on established customs value

**Currency and Payment:**
- All values expressed in USD
- Exchange rate applied: 1 USD = 24 pesos
- Recipient pays 30% of total value
- Payment in local currency

**Reference:** https://avsup.info/app/services.php

### Payment Requirements at Delivery

**General Rule:**
- Yes, payment required at delivery (unless pre-arranged with sales office)

**What Recipients Pay:**
- Customs tariffs for product nationalization
- Destination agency service fees
- Presented via invoice at delivery

**Payment Options:**
1. **All-Inclusive**: Some agencies include tariffs with shipping cost
2. **Separate Payment**: Other agencies charge only shipping; recipient pays tariffs and services in local currency at delivery

**Reference:** https://avsup.info/app/services.php

### Tariff-Exempt Articles

**Completely Exempt Products:**
1. Food, medicines, and personal hygiene products
   - Must ship in separate packages
   - Maximum weight: 20kg
   
2. Miscellaneous package up to 3kg maximum weight

3. Single appliance with customs value of $30 USD or less

4. Photovoltaic solutions including solar panels

**Reference:** https://avsup.info/app/aduanalist.php

- Time for cargo to connect with airlines and arrive in Cuba

**Important Factors:**

- Force majeure events may cause delays:
  - Weather phenomena
  - Flight cancellations
  - Other unforeseen circumstances

### Delivery Time After Arrival in Cuba

**Estimated Timeline:**

**Customs Processing:**
- Normal review and nationalization: 5-7 days

**Distribution:**
- Completion of delivery: 14-21 days

**Total Average:**
- Recipient should receive package within 21-40 days after arrival in Cuba

**Tracking:** https://avsup.info/app/trackingawb.php

### Door-to-Door Delivery Service

**Available Locations:**
- **Door-to-door service**: Only available for capital city deliveries

**Other Provinces and Municipalities:**
- Delivery organized at nearest available point to recipient location
- Recipient notified in advance
- Appointment scheduled for pickup at agreed location

**Note:** Review delivery details with dispatch agency based on destination

**Conditions for Alternate Recipient:**

1. **Notification**: Contact dispatch agency to request recipient update
2. **Identification**: Provide ID of person who will receive package (verified at delivery)
3. **If Cargo Not Yet Shipped**: Documents reprinted, labels updated
4. **If Cargo Already Shipped**: Wait for customs clearance, then update recipient during distribution phase

### Recipient Phone Number Changed

**Procedure:**

1. **Notification**: Contact dispatch agency to request phone number update
2. **If Cargo Not Yet Shipped**: Documents reprinted, labels updated
3. **If Cargo Already Shipped**: Wait for customs clearance, then update contact number during distribution phase

### Delayed Package

**If Delivery Exceeds Expected Time:**

1. **Notification**: Contact dispatch agency about delivery delays
2. **Maximum Timeline**: All customers should receive packages within 30 days of cargo arrival in Cuba
3. **Force Majeure Events**: Natural disasters or infrastructure damage may cause longer delays

## 8. Regulations and Sanctions

### In-Person Clearance (Despacho Presencial)

**What It Means:**
- Package contents flagged for physical inspection by customs or border control agents
- Purpose: Determine if items are permitted for country entry
- Process conducted jointly with cargo consignee

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Satisfactory**: Items cleared for entry
- **Confiscation**: Unauthorized items confiscated

**If Items Confiscated:**
- Can file complaint with customs customer service system
- Customs decision is final

### Product Confiscation or Inspection

**Reasons for Confiscation:**
- Failure to comply with permitted items regulations
- Quantities suggesting commercial intent

**Process:**
1. Affected recipient cited for in-person cargo review
2. If confiscation applied, can file complaint with customs customer service
3. Customs verdict is final

**Prevention:**
- Always consult current import regulations before shipping
- Reference: https://avsup.info/app/aduanalist.php

## 10. Product List, Permitted Quantities, and Customs Values

### General Import Principles

**Customs Authority Requirements:**
Articles and products admitted for import by individuals must:

1. Correspond to non-commercial importation
2. Be declared transparently with varied quantities
3. Not exceed established limits
4. Nature, function, or import repetition must not indicate commercial intent

**Violation Consequences:**
- Customs may determine commercial character
- Sanctions applied per customs regulations

### Miscellaneous Classification

**Items Classified as Miscellaneous:**
- Footwear
- Clothing/confections
- Food
- Personal and household hygiene items
- Costume jewelry
- Perfumery and similar items

**Requirements:**
- Items must be diverse in types
- Commercial character determined by lack of diversity or repetition of same item type
- Excess quantities: Customs admits up to permitted weight, confiscates remainder

### Specific Regulations

**VEHICLES:**
- **Prohibited**: Complete motor vehicles, vehicle engines, vehicle bodies
- **Permitted**: Parts and accessories (quantity must not exceed one vehicle's repair/maintenance needs)
- Subject to specific legislation

**FISHING EQUIPMENT:**
- Up to 3 fishing line spools/reels/bobbins
- Up to 10kg of hooks, weights, lures, and similar items

**BICYCLES AND SIMILAR:**
- Up to 2 units: bicycles, electric bicycles, pedal-assist bicycles, electric scooters, or similar

**PNEUMATIC WEAPONS:**
- 4.5mm or smaller projectiles
- Must meet legal requirements
- 1 unit per person, one-time only
- Minimum reference value: $500 USD

**TOOLS:**
- Valuation methods: reference value, declaration, or purchase invoice
- Up to 2 of each type accepted
- Hand tools under 3kg may use value-weight method
- Tools for commercial, industrial, or manufacturing activities not admitted as non-commercial import

**FURNITURE:**
- Up to 5 units of each type valued at $50 USD or less
- Up to 3 units of each type exceeding $50 USD (including sets)
- Beds/cribs: respective mattresses admitted
- Baby furniture (cribs, strollers, playpens, rockers, walkers, similar): up to 3 units of each type

**HARDWARE:**
- Non-durable household/hardware items and low-value durable items: value-weight alternative used
- Up to 5 units of each type valued at $50 USD or less
- Up to 3 units exceeding $50 USD (including sets)

**PAINTS AND FINISHES:**
- Paints, varnishes, pigments, thinners
- Total container contents must not exceed 20 liters (regardless of container format)

**ELECTRICAL CABLES:**
- Up to 3 spools/reels of up to 100 meters each

**HOSES:**
- For gardening, washing, similar uses
- Total up to 50 meters

**CARPETS:**
- Total up to 50 square meters

**CONSTRUCTION MATERIALS:**
- Flooring/wall cladding pieces: up to 50 m²
- Trim moldings, borders, similar: up to 50 linear meters
- Construction use manufactures: up to 50 units
- Hardware and other construction materials: up to 10 units

## 12. Sanitary Regulations for Food Importation

### Regulatory Authority
Sanitary regulations established by the National Center for Animal Health (CENASA) of the Ministry of Agriculture, based on international standards approved by the World Organization for Animal Health (OIE).

### Purpose
Prevent introduction of biological agents that cause diseases harmful to:
- Domestic animals
- Wild fauna
- Humans (in case of zoonoses)

### Absolute Import Prohibitions

**Prohibited Products:**

1. **Fresh, frozen, dried, or salted meats** (with or without bones)
   - Edible organs
   - Semi-processed meat products:
     - Hamburgers
     - Ground meat
     - Meatballs
     - Formed products (meat sticks, nuggets, breaded products)
     - Raw sausages
     - Salami
     - Spreadable pastes
     - Raw ham
     - Raw sausages
     - Raw bacon
     - Fermented products
     - Sobreasada
     - Pepperoni
     - Cervelat
     - Other raw fermented products
   - Semi-cooked products

2. **Any animal product or byproduct with bones**

3. **Fluid milk and dairy products that are NOT PASTEURIZED**

### Import Facilities (Permitted Items)

**1. Canned Meat Products (Bovine, Pork, Poultry)**

**Requirements:**
- Properly identified
- Recognized commercial brands
- From eligible countries based on current zoosanitary situation and agreements between Official Veterinary Services

**Eligible Countries:**
- Spain
- Portugal
- Italy
- United States
- Canada
- Mexico
- Panama
- Costa Rica
- Nicaragua
- Brazil
- Argentina
- Chile
- Uruguay

**2. Sausages (Embutidos)**

**Status:** Temporarily authorized

**Requirements:**
- From eligible countries (same list as canned meat products)

**Eligible Countries:**
- Spain
- Portugal
- Italy
- United States
- Canada
- Mexico
- Panama
- Costa Rica
- Nicaragua
- Brazil
- Argentina
- Chile
- Uruguay

**3. Seafood Products**
- Canned seafood from any geographic area permitted

**4. Fluid and Processed Milk**

**Permitted Types:**
- UHT fluid milk
- Condensed milk
- Evaporated milk
- Dairy desserts

**Origin:** Any geographic area

**5. Powdered Milk**
- Origin: Any geographic area

**6. Cheeses**

**Permitted Types:**
- Mature pasteurized cheeses from any geographic area
- Cheeses made from ultra-pasteurized milk

### Important Notes

**Country Eligibility:**
- Based on country where products were manufactured or produced
- Country of origin reflected on product labels

**Packaging Requirements:**
- All products must arrive fully packaged
- Recognized brands
- Properly labeled
- No broken packaging

**Damaged Packaging:**
- Products with broken or deteriorated packaging may become carriers of disease-causing etiological agents
- Will be confiscated and incinerated
